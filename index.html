Who He Is

The Prodigy
of Shadows

Itachi Uchiha was a shinobi of Konohagakure's Uchiha clan — a prodigy who graduated the Academy at age 7, mastered the Sharingan at 8, became a Chūnin at 10, and joined the elite ANBU Black Ops at 13.

He was celebrated as a genius even by Uchiha standards — a man whose mastery of genjutsu, ninjutsu, and taijutsu was unmatched in his generation. Yet beneath the glory lay a burden no child should ever carry.

Tasked by Konoha's leadership to prevent a coup that would plunge the world into war, Itachi made an impossible choice — slaughter his entire clan, and carry the curse of a murderer for the rest of his days.

Chronicle

The Life of a
Cursed Genius

Age 4
Witness to War
At just four years old, Itachi witnessed the brutal aftermath of the Third Shinobi World War. The death and suffering scarred him deeply, making him a pacifist at heart before he was even old enough to wield a kunai.
Age 7
Academy Graduation
Itachi graduated the Ninja Academy at age 7 — a feat that normally takes years of disciplined study. He had already begun to surpass shinobi twice his age, and the village whispered of a once-in-a-generation talent.
Age 8
Sharingan Awakening
After witnessing the death of his best friend Shisui Uchiha, Itachi's grief awakened the Sharingan — and soon after, the Mangekyō Sharingan. The loss that shaped his power would haunt him for the rest of his life.
Age 13
ANBU Captain
By 13, Itachi had risen to Captain of the ANBU Black Ops — the youngest in Konoha's history. He served as both its deadliest weapon and most conflicted conscience, simultaneously feeding intelligence to the Hokage about his own clan.
Age 13
The Night of the Massacre
Acting on orders from Danzō Shimura and the Konoha elders — and in alliance with Obito Uchiha — Itachi killed every member of the Uchiha clan in a single night. He spared one life: his younger brother Sasuke, whom he loved above all else.
Age 13–17
Life as Akatsuki
To complete his cover, Itachi joined the Akatsuki — the world's most feared criminal organisation. In truth, he served as a double agent for Konoha, feeding information while slowly dying from a debilitating illness he refused to treat.
Age 18
Final Battle & Death
Itachi faced Sasuke in their fated confrontation. Though disease had stolen most of his vision and his life, Itachi fought on — not to kill Sasuke, but to transplant his eyes and free Sasuke from Orochimaru's cursed seal. His final act was a gentle tap on Sasuke's forehead. "Sorry, Sasuke. This is the last time."

The pinnacle of Uchiha ocular power. Itachi's Mangekyō Sharingan grants access to Tsukuyomi, Amaterasu, and Susanoo — three invincible techniques that place him among the most dangerous shinobi who ever lived.
月
Tsukuyomi
月読
A nightmarish genjutsu that traps its victim in a mental dimension where Itachi controls time itself. 72 hours of torture pass in the span of a single second. Its victims are left catatonic, their minds shattered beyond repair.
天
Amaterasu
天照
Black flames summoned from Itachi's left eye that burn at 7,000°C and cannot be extinguished by any force. They burn without oxygen, without end — consuming everything the eye perceives until nothing remains but ash.
須
Susanoo
須佐能乎
A colossal spectral warrior that fully envelops its user. Itachi's Susanoo wields the Sword of Totsuka — a weapon that seals anything it pierces into an eternal dreamlike genjutsu — and the Yata Mirror, said to be invincible against all elements.
幻
Genjutsu Mastery
幻術
Itachi was widely regarded as the greatest genjutsu user of his generation. He could cast multiple layered illusions simultaneously — trapping enemies in an endless loop of escaping one nightmare, only to enter another.
火
Fire Release
火遁・豪火球の術
A master of fire-based ninjutsu, Itachi could unleash the Grand Fireball Technique — a blazing sphere of white-hot flame — as well as the Phoenix Sage Fire Technique, which launches volleys of smaller guided fireballs capable of melting rock.

Cultural Impact

What the World
Thinks of Him

Itachi Uchiha is considered one of the greatest anime characters ever written. His story isn't just a tale of tragedy — it's a masterclass in moral complexity, unconditional love, and the weight of impossible choices. Fans around the world wept when they finally understood the truth behind his eyes.

The Final Truth

"He was never a villain.
He was a god who bled
for those who hated him."

Itachi Uchiha lived and died for a village that called him a murderer. He endured the hatred of the brother he adored, the contempt of the world he served, and the deterioration of his own body — all to maintain the peace he had witnessed the cost of as a four-year-old child.

He is not simply a fictional character. He is a meditation on sacrifice — on what it means to love someone so much that you become the monster in their story so they can be the hero.

The crows scatter. The genjutsu fades. And what remains, in the quiet after the credits roll, is the unbearable weight of understanding.
